Spaced repetition is a learning technique that reviews information at increasing intervals, leveraging the spacing effect to strengthen long-term memory.

## How It Works

- Review new material frequently at first
- Gradually increase the interval between reviews
- Reset the interval if recall fails

## Connection to Note-Taking

Writing permanent notes is itself a form of spaced repetition. Each time you revisit and refine a note, you reinforce the underlying concept.
